What is a Hip to Gable Loft Conversion?

A Hip to Gable Loft Conversion is a popular way to extend a loft space in homes with a hipped roof. This involves replacing the sloping (hipped) roof on one or both sides of the house with a vertical (gable) wall. This transformation increases the internal loft space, making it more suitable for a functional room such as a bedroom, office, or living area.

Types of Hip to Gable Loft Conversions

There are several types of hip to gable loft conversions to suit different property styles

1. Standard Hip to Gable Conversion

This involves converting one side of a hipped roof into a gable end, suitable for semi-detached or end-terrace houses. It provides more space without drastically altering the home's exterior.

Learn More
2. Double Hip to Gable Conversion

For detached homes, both hipped sides can be converted to gable ends, maximizing loft space and creating a much larger living area. This type of conversion is ideal for homeowners looking for significant additional space.

Learn More
3. Hip to Gable with Rear Dormer

This combination extends the loft even further by adding a rear dormer to the new gable end, creating even more usable space. It is particularly useful when more headroom and additional windows are required.

Learn More
4. Hip to Gable with Velux Windows

Rather than adding a dormer, this option includes large skylights (Velux windows) to bring in natural light while maintaining a sleek roofline.

Learn More

Key Factors Affecting Cost

Several factors impact the total cost of a hip to gable loft conversion:

Property Type & Size

Larger homes require more materials and labor, which can increase costs.

Structural Changes

Reinforcing floors, roofing adjustments, and new walls affect pricing.

Windows & Skylights

Adding Velux windows, dormers, or Juliet balconies can increase costs.

Finishing & Interiors

Plumbing, electrics, insulation, and decoration contribute to the final budget.

Planning & Permissions

Some projects may require planning permission and additional fees.

Accessibility & Complexity

If your property has restricted access, additional costs may be incurred for scaffolding and transportation of materials.

Planning Permission & Building Regulations

Most hip to gable loft conversions fall under permitted development rights, meaning planning permission may not be required. However, in conservation areas or for extensive modifications, permission may be needed.

Key Regulations:
Building Regulations Approval

Ensures structural safety, insulation, and fire safety compliance.

Party Wall Agreement

Needed if the work affects shared walls in a semi-detached or terraced home.

Height & Roof Structure

Loft space must have a minimum head height of 2.2m for practical conversion.

Fire Safety Measures

Installation of fire doors, smoke alarms, and safe escape routes is mandatory.

Sound Insulation

Required in semi-detached and terraced properties to reduce noise transfer between homes.

How Long Does a Hip to Gable Loft Conversion Take?

A hip to gable loft conversion typically takes 6 to 10 weeks, depending on complexity, weather, and customization.

Weeks 1-2

Initial setup, scaffolding, and preparation of the roof area.

Weeks 3-5

Major structural work, creating the gable wall and roof modifications.

Weeks 6-8

Internal work including plumbing, electrics, insulation, and plastering.

Weeks 9-10

Finishing touches, decorating, fixtures, and final inspections.
