Frequently Asked Questions

A dormer loft conversion typically takes 6 to 10 weeks, depending on the project's complexity. Prefab options may be completed faster. We provide a clear timeline at the start of the project to keep everything on track.

In many cases, a dormer loft conversion falls under permitted development, meaning you don't need planning permission. However, if your home is in a conservation area or the conversion exceeds certain size limits, you may need approval. We handle all necessary paperwork to ensure your project meets legal requirements.

To ensure comfort and usability, the ceiling height for a dormer loft conversion should ideally be at least 2.2 meters (7 feet 3 inches). This allows enough headroom for a functional living space. If your loft is slightly lower, we can explore options such as lowering the floor level to meet requirements.

Most homes can accommodate a dormer loft conversion, but key factors include the existing loft height (at least 2.2 meters is recommended), the roof structure and pitch, available space for a staircase, and planning restrictions in your area. We offer free assessments to check if your home is suitable.
